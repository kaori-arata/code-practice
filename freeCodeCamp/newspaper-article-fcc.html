Autonomous Delivery Drones Launch in City Center, Transforming Logistics History

Advanced AI ensures safety, marking the dawn of a new era with zero traffic and 5-minute delivery times

By Ren Kanzaki

Today, ITS Technology officially launched "Sky Delivery," a commercial service featuring next-generation autonomous drones equipped with artificial intelligence across the metropolitan area. While drone deliveries were previously limited to experimental test zones, revisions to aviation laws and the introduction of a new traffic management system have finally made the service available to the general public. Users can now simply place an order through a smartphone application to receive their packages at their balconies or designated landing pads from the nearest hub in as little as five minutes.

The defining feature of this service is its proprietary obstacle-avoidance AI, named "Sentinel Eye." The system predicts collisions with power lines, birds, and other drones in real-time, completely automating route recalculations. The operating company expressed strong confidence, stating that this technology keeps the accident rate lower than that of traditional truck transportation. As the logistics industry grapples with chronic driver shortages and urban traffic congestion, great expectations are being placed on this "sky infrastructure" to serve as a vital savior.
